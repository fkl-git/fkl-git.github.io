About

I am a student at UWC Thailand interested in the structure and legitimacy of global institutions.

My work focuses on a central question: how do systems justify their authority—and what happens when that justification begins to fail?

This question has led me to explore international law, particularly the tension between sovereignty and universal accountability. I am especially interested in how legal frameworks present themselves as neutral while operating within deeply unequal global structures.

Alongside this, I engage in research, writing, and academic competitions that emphasize argumentation, clarity, and critical thinking.

I see my work not as a collection of achievements, but as an ongoing attempt to understand how power operates—and how it can be challenged.
