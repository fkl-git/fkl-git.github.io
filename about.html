About

I am a student at UWC Thailand interested in the structure, legitimacy, and failure points of global institutions.

My work is shaped by one recurring question: how do systems justify their authority, and what happens when that justification begins to fail? This has led me toward international law, political economy, fiscal governance, and the broader tension between sovereignty and accountability.

I am especially interested in how legal and economic frameworks present themselves as neutral while operating inside unequal global structures. Through research, writing, debate, and student leadership, I try to make complex public issues more precise, more readable, and more accountable.

I see my work not as a collection of achievements, but as an ongoing attempt to understand how power operates — and how it can be challenged.
